The Spark that Traveled the World

On a cold October night in 1960, a young presidential candidate named John F. Kennedy stood on the steps of the University of Michigan. He didn't give a typical political speech. Instead, he asked the thousands of students waiting for him: "How many of you are willing to spend your lives traveling around the world to help others?"

This question was the "spark." By March 1, 1961, that spark became the Peace Corps. Kennedy believed that an idea could live on long after a person is gone. He wanted to build camaraderie between nations not through weapons, but through compassion.

Today, volunteers like 84-year-old Ralph Bernstein prove that age is just a number. While Ralph teaches science in Ghana, others work on the "Let Girls Learn" initiative to empower young women. Grammar Focus: Adverbs of Time

When using multiple adverbs of time, we follow a specific order:

1. How Long? → 2. How Often? → 3. When?

Example: "I volunteered for two months every summer last year."
